Fish Food... For Thought

Clownfish (Amphiprioninae)

Clownfish are small, colorful fish that are popular in home aquariums. The average length of a clownfish is between 2 to 4 inches. They are omnivores and feed on a variety of foods including algae, plankton, and small crustaceans. In the wild, clownfish can be found in warm, shallow waters of the Pacific and Indian Oceans, particularly around coral reefs.

Hippo Blue Tang (Paracanthurus hepatus)

Hippo Blue Tangs, also known as regal tangs, are known for their striking blue coloration and are popular in home aquariums. The average length of a Hippo Blue Tang is around 12 inches. They are herbivores and feed on seaweed and algae. In the wild, they can be found in the Indo-Pacific region, particularly around coral reefs.

Moorish Idol Fish (Zanclus cornutus)

Moorish Idol Fish are known for their distinctive black, white, and yellow coloring and are popular in home aquariums. The average length of a Moorish Idol is around 8 inches. They are omnivores and feed on a variety of foods including algae, plankton, and small crustaceans. In the wild, they can be found in the warm waters of the Pacific Ocean, particularly around coral reefs.

Threadfin Butterflyfish (Chaetodon auriga)

Threadfin Butterflyfish are known for their distinctive thread-like fins and peaceful nature, and are popular in home aquariums. The average length of a Threadfin Butterflyfish is around 6 inches. They are omnivores and feed on a variety of foods including flakes, pellets, and frozen or live foods such as brine shrimp and bloodworms. In the wild, they can be found in the warm waters of the Indo-Pacific region, particularly around coral reefs.

Royal Angelfish (Pygoplites diacanthus)

Royal Angelfish are known for their striking blue and yellow coloration and are popular in home aquariums. The average length of a Royal Angelfish is around 10 inches. They are omnivores and feed on a variety of foods including algae, plankton, and small invertebrates. In the wild, they can be found in the warm waters of the Indo-Pacific region, particularly around coral reefs.

Sea Star (Asteroidea)

Sea stars, also known as starfish, come in a variety of colors and sizes and can be a unique addition to a home aquarium. The average length of a sea star can vary greatly depending on the species. They are carnivorous and feed on a variety of foods including small invertebrates and decaying organic matter. In the wild, they can be found in all of the world's oceans, from the shallow intertidal zone to the deep sea.
